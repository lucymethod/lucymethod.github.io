History
Lucy Method is grounded on experience of modern humans and research papers dealt with the first humans.
It embraces the lifestyle of homo sapience of the period nor less then 3.2 million years.
That is the age of Lucy.
Lucy is a collection of fossilized bones which were discovered in Ethiopia in 1974.
The bones made up a skeleton of the hominin species Australopithecus afarensis.
Lucy is the most important fossile ever discovered in archeology.
The assembly showed that Lucy moved upright.
By studying her bones and in particular the structure of her knee and spine curvature.
Scientist were able to discover that she spent most of her time moving on two legs, like humans.
The method has been elaborated with as much simplicity of this prehistoric experience as possible.

Back to the movement
Lucy Method combines physical exercises and otherworldliness editations.
Moreover combined with knowledge of nutrition and livestyles.
As Lucy was the first hominid the main idea of the method is “back to the movement”.
The method proves that people should look back through millions of years and become opportunistic movers again.
It should be done for many reasons and the main one is to heal our bodies.
Our locomotion is tough and it doesn’t give us the opportunity to expand freedom of movement in our ordinary habitual life.
Lucy Method combines all the possible ways of expressing freedom of our body, where limitation are causing health problems.
